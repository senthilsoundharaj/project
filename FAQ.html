Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the ideal pH for drinking water?
The ideal pH for drinking water is between **6.5 and 8.5**. Water within this range is considered safe and neutral.
How does pH affect plant growth?
pH affects nutrient availability in the soil. Most plants grow best in a pH range of **6.0 to 7.0**. Outside this range, essential nutrients may become less available.
How can I lower the pH of my water naturally?
You can lower water pH naturally by adding **vinegar**, **citric acid**, or using **peat moss** in your water filtration system.
What plants grow best in acidic soil?
Plants like **blueberries**, **azaleas**, **hydrangeas**, and **rhododendrons** thrive in acidic soil (pH **5.0 to 6.0**).
How often should I test water pH?
For gardening, test water pH **every 2–4 weeks**. For drinking water, test it **every 6 months** or if you notice changes in taste or quality.
